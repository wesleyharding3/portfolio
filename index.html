SYSTEM 01// LIVE BUSINESS · FULL-STACK SAAS

Showroom Inventory

A multi-tenant SaaS for fashion showrooms. Every client brand is a sandboxed organization; pieces carry QR codes; a phone scan checks a garment in or out — recording who has it, why, and when it's due back. The QR binder and the hangers are the source of truth, and the database mirrors them exactly. No native app, no App Store.

Real inventory — America 250 collection
Stack Next.js 15 · Supabase (Postgres·Auth·Storage·RLS) · Stripe · Vercel Repo github.com/wesleyharding3/wtf Role Solo — design, DB, backend, frontend, infra
THE THESIS // PHYSICAL ↔ DIGITAL SOURCE OF TRUTH

The digital organization mirrors the physical one — linked at a single hinge.

Each piece's tag_id — one unique string, abstracted across qr/nfc/rfid so the tag tech can change without a re-architecture — is the exact value its QR encodes and the primary key a scan resolves. The same id prints onto the binder card and the hanger tag, so the rack and the record can never drift. An append-only ledger is the temporal source of truth; the item's status is just a cache the scan functions keep in sync.

FULL-STACK BREADTH// ONE ENGINEER, EVERY LAYER
01
Database & backend
Tenant isolation in the database

9 tables, 12 enums, 28 RLS policies and 11 PL/pgSQL functions across 13 migrations. Isolation is enforced in Postgres — not the app layer — so it holds no matter which query reaches the data. Anti-escalation triggers block privilege changes; a single requireSuperadmin gate guards the only RLS-bypassing client.

02
Network & infra
Hardened by configuration

Security headers on every route (2-year HSTS, frame & sniff guards), an open-redirect sanitizer that defeats the tab/newline-normalization bypass, edge middleware that refreshes sessions and guards routes, and three isolated Supabase clients with the service-role key fenced server-only.

03
Front-end & dashboards
Three personas, one shell

One route renders three completely different products: the operator ops console (provisioning, a hand-built month calendar, stylist roster), the designer brand portal (read-only collection + earned-media), and the stylist studio. A bespoke 417-line CSS system — no Tailwind, no UI kit — plus hand-rolled charts, 3D flip-cards, and a WebGL auth logo.

04
Payments & integrations
Real money, verified

Stripe Checkout for a one-time stylist onboarding fee with the card saved on file, fulfilled by a signature-verified webhook that recomputes amounts server-side. Supabase auth (magic-link, invite, password), plus ExcelJS exports and a pandas importer that ingested 246 real press placements across 14 brands.

05
Physical systems
Binder cards & hanger tags

In-house QR generation (no SaaS) renders the same tag_id into two print formats — index-card binder pages and small hanger tags — color-coded per brand. A batch endpoint mints up to 2,000 codes at once, each RLS-scoped so a brand can only print its own.

06
Business optimization
Loans, closed back to PR

A check-out's purpose (editorial, runway, celebrity/VIP…) links the loan to the earned coverage it produced — so a press report can show a piece worn for a shoot, the borrower, and the return date. Tier-weighted, time-decayed earned-media-value ranking turns coverage into a measurable ROI story.

THE ANCHOR// A MULTILINGUAL NLP PIPELINE

Most of earth00 exists to serve one hard problem: read the same story in any language and connect it to every other story about it. That means tokenizing scripts the naive way can't (CJK, Arabic, Thai), translating keywords to a canonical form so Пекин · 北京 · بكين all collapse to beijing, scoring sentiment without an API, resolving named entities against Wikidata, and embedding every article into a vector space so semantically-near coverage clusters even when it shares no words.

Extract
Script-aware keywords

Unigrams + bigrams with a 3× title boost, Unicode-range language detection, CJK/Thai segmentation. ~48-language stopword & common-word prefilter cuts ~45% of downstream LLM calls.

Unify
Cross-lingual keywords

Claude Haiku normalizes non-English keywords to canonical English so a story clusters across languages — DeepL is a cost-gated fallback. ~$1.80/day at steady state.

Resolve
Entities & knowledge graph

Claude extracts people/orgs/places + referenced historical dates; a three-layer cascade resolves them against a ~21k local gazetteer, then Wikidata, then the LLM.

Embed
Local vector space

A CPU transformer (384-dim, 50+ langs, $0/run) embeds each article into pgvector, powering k-NN clustering, sentiment, and a rule-based ~48-language lexicon.
